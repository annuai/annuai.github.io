    A Refresh to indicator-stickynotes
    11 Jun 2015
    1 minute to read

    I love good looking apps.As a budding UI designer, I should.I was a chronic user of Sticky Notes on Windows, when I had been a Windows User.Sticky Notes helped me save a lot of paper and got me to memorize my things in time.

    When I switched to Linux about a year ago, the major problem was nothing but the deficiency of apps available.Sticky Notes happened to be one of them.

    I searched and searched…and searched and found the only app I could use was Indicator-Stickynotes by Umang Varma.It is a good alternative, but still doesn’t look as good as others.

    I just thought, why not give it a refresh, it is a good app, I can make it even better.

    I just used some free icons and font and some neat tricks to make this work.

    And I hope this works for you too.

    I have made a bash script, so that all you need to do is to run it.

    Download the refresh

    NB: You should’ve installed indicator-stickynotes from Umang Varma’s PPA
           Also, Backup all your notes before proceeding, the bash file will reset all your notes.

    After Downloading, extract it to stickynotes-refresh-master folder in home

    cd ~/stickynotes-refresh-master
sudo chmod +x setup.sh
./setup.sh

    That’s all folks!
